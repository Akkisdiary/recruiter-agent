Senior Full-Stack Engineer
Stripe — San Francisco, CA (Remote eligible)

About the role

We're looking for a Senior Full-Stack Engineer to join our Payments team. You'll work on the core systems that move money for millions of businesses worldwide. You'll build and maintain APIs, design user-facing dashboards, and collaborate closely with product and design to ship features that directly impact Stripe's revenue.

What you'll do

- Design, build, and maintain scalable web applications and APIs that handle thousands of requests per second
- Work across the stack — from React frontends to Python/Ruby backend services to PostgreSQL and Redis data stores
- Own features end-to-end: scoping, architecture, implementation, testing, deployment, and monitoring
- Collaborate with product managers, designers, and other engineers in a fast-paced Agile environment
- Improve engineering practices: code reviews, testing strategies, CI/CD pipelines, and observability
- Mentor junior engineers and contribute to technical design discussions
- Debug and resolve production incidents, ensuring 99.99% uptime for payment-critical systems

What we look for

- 4+ years of professional software engineering experience
- Strong proficiency in at least one backend language (Python, Ruby, Java, or Go) and one frontend framework (React, Vue, or Angular)
- Experience designing and building RESTful APIs
- Solid understanding of relational databases (PostgreSQL, MySQL) and caching layers (Redis, Memcached)
- Experience with cloud infrastructure (AWS, GCP, or Azure) and containerization (Docker, Kubernetes)
- Familiarity with CI/CD tools (GitHub Actions, Jenkins, CircleCI)
- Strong communication skills and ability to work cross-functionally
- BS or MS in Computer Science or equivalent practical experience

Nice to have

- Experience with payment systems or fintech
- Familiarity with event-driven architectures (Kafka, RabbitMQ)
- Experience with observability tools (Datadog, Prometheus, Grafana)
- Contributions to open-source projects
- Experience with TypeScript and Node.js
